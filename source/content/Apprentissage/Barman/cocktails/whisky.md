Le whisky est produits à partir de grains aged in wooden barrels, and subject to regional regulations that define its production and style.Les types de whisky sont :
- **[[Rye]] (Seigle)**
- **[[Bourbon]]** 



##### Différent whiskey
- Irish
- Scotch
- Japonais
- Américain: [[rye]], [[Bourbon]], Tennesee
- single malt


###### Scotch
Toujours produit en Écosse: Highland, Islay, Speyside, Lowlands, and Campbeltown, each with distinct flavor profiles.

Fait d'une proportion d'orge maltée, d'orge non maltée et d'autres grains. 
- Le blended Scotch contient au moins 20 % de whisky de malt,
- tandis que les single malts sont faits exclusivement à partir d'orge maltée. 

Le Scotch est vieilli dans des fûts légèrement toastés, ce qui lui donne une saveur moins intense. 
- **Flavor Profile**: Often smoky, derived from peat fires used to dry malt.



 ###### **Tennessee Whiskey**
- **Region**: Tennessee, USA.
- **Unique Process**: Undergoes the **Lincoln County Process**, 
	- filtering through sugar maple charcoal for smoothness.
- **Flavor Profile**: Mellow, with a smoother edge compared to bourbon.


###### **Single Malt Whiskey**
- **Definition**: Made from one malted grain at a single distillery.
- **Regions**: Commonly produced in Scotland (Scotch), Japan, Ireland, and the United States.
- **Flavor Profile**: Varies widely based on region and distillation process.


###### **Irish Whiskey**
- **Region**: Ireland.
- **Mash Bill**: Often unmalted barley; some single malts also produced.
- **Flavor Profile**: Light with a short finish, though pot-still variations are richer.

###### **Japanese Whiskey**
- **Region**: Japan.
- **Inspiration**: Modeled after Scotch, typically made from malted barley and aged in unique wood casks (e.g., mizunara oak).
- **Flavor Profile**: Distinct, often influenced by Japanese oak and regional techniques.


###### Spelling distinctions of the "e"
- **With "e"**: Whiskey (USA, Ireland).
- **Without "e"**: Whisky (Scotland, Japan, Canada).
